# dishmatch.github.io
**Problem**
Finding what to eat is a challenge many people face day to day, wherever or whenever they are,
whether it be at home or abroad, and they need it later or right away. In personal experience,
the way people around me usually search for food was by going through a browser or
DoorDash/Uber Eats, looking at multiple options with less intuitive options/filters to narrow them
down for options past ordering out.

**Solution**
I designed Dishmatch, a mobile app that allows users the flexibility to quickly find food around
them with filters and a search capability centered around people’s usual first thoughts on what
they're looking for.

**How did you go about translating/addressing the need? What was your process?**
I addressed the need by analyzing competitors in the field, like Google, DoorDash, and Uber
Eats, and recognized where their apps fell short in terms of helping users find spots to eat
beyond takeout and packaging it into an easy-to-navigate UI. I followed the design trend of a
dating app like Hinge to reflect the easy scrollable and intuitive nature of filtering what a user
wants and positioning matches as restaurants, allowing users to filter their “matches” in the
same way people would filter their love interests. My concept would allow users to quickly filter
restaurants with a focus on filtering by aspects like cuisine, distance, establishment (sitdown,
takeout, foodtruck, etc), and save (match) or remove them from their feed as people would use
a dating app.
